Creative Insider

Creative Insider was to become a Behance style web portal for artists, designers, photographers and other creative professionals. Unfortunately, the project was put on hold indefinitely.

The Brief

An extensive and ambitious project, Creative Insider was from the beginning and it excited us all. The scope of work was huge and we meticuluosly prepared ourselves for the job. Client handling and meetings were attended by the project manager and senior designer (that was me). Client breifing were noted down, minutes of the meeting were passed on to the senior management, and design parameters discussed. This was the first large project that I had undertaken back then.

Design

Initial wireframes (sent by client) were only sufficient to an extent. We had thought about all aspects to cover through design. The users' need to like, share, and recommend, not to mention add more projects and individual artworks were to be cleverly incorporated in each design. The team began with devising the information architecture. We used sticky notes for this purpose. My first paper and pen prototype. Handling such a big project was only possible if a dedicated team is involved. Thanks to each and every member of my team we were able to prepare wireframes without missing out on any critical element.

The lessons learnt from this project are plenty. Here, I put strong emphasis on the documentation part. Any big project's success depends on strong documentation.
